## Abstract
We rethink what it means for AI to be proactive in multi-party conversations. Rather than merely reacting to turn-taking cues, a proactive AI formulates its own inner thoughts during a conversation, and seeks the right moment to contribute. Through a formative study with 24 participants, we introduce the Inner Thoughts framework, equipping AI with a continuous covert train of thoughts parallel to overt communication, modeling intrinsic motivation to express these thoughts. User studies show our framework significantly surpasses baselines on anthropomorphism, coherence, intelligence, and turn-taking appropriateness.

## Key Contributions
- Rethinks proactivity in multi-party conversations beyond reactive turn-taking
- Introduces Inner Thoughts framework with continuous covert thoughts parallel to overt communication
- Models intrinsic motivation for AI to formulate and express thoughts
- Formative study with 24 participants informing framework design
- Significantly surpasses baselines on anthropomorphism, coherence, intelligence, turn-taking appropriateness
