## Summary

3GPP TS 28.556 defines the Stage 2 (information model) and Stage 3 (solution set) specifications for the Policy Management Service (Policy MnS) in 5G mobile networks. It covers policy lifecycle management procedures — creation, deletion, update, query, activation, deactivation — and conflict notification, along with RESTful HTTP-based and YANG/NETCONF-based solution sets for implementation.

## Key Claims

- Policy MnS uses generic provisioning MnS operations (createMOI, deleteMOI, modifyMOIAttributes, getMOIAttributes) defined in TS 28.532 for policy lifecycle management
- The Policy IOC inherits from the Top IOC (TS 28.622) and includes four mandatory attributes: policyPriority, policyStatus, policyType, and policyContent
- PolicyContent is a dataType composed of condition and action, representing the core logic of a network policy
- Policy conflict detection occurs when a new or updated policy conflicts with previously stored policies; conflicts are notified via notifyPolicyConflict
- Policy priority values are LOW/Medium/High (ENUM); status values are ACTIVATED/DEACTIVATED (ENUM, default DEACTIVATED)
- Stage 3 provides RESTful HTTP mapping (PUT/GET/PATCH/DELETE) and YANG/NETCONF solution sets for policy operations
- The notifyPolicyConflict notification follows ITU-T X.733 alarm reporting conventions, including objectClass, objectInstance, notificationId, eventTime, systemDN, and conflictDescription parameters

## Key Quotes

> "The operations (e.g. createMOI operations) and notifications (e.g. notifyMOIcreation) of generic provisioning MnS defined in TS 28.532 [4] can be used for policy lifecycle management. The policy can be treated as object instance." — Section 5.1

> "This notification supports the policy conflict notification to be notified when a new policy conflicts with the previous storage policy." — Section 5.2.3.2.1.1

> "conflictDetails shall convey more information about the conflict. Description of the detected policy conflicts, e.g. conflicting events, conditions or actions among the policies." — Section 5.2.3.2.1.2
