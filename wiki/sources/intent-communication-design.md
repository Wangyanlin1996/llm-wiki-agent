## Summary
This paper introduces a multidimensional design space for intent communication along three dimensions: Transparency (what), Abstraction (when), and Modality (how), applied to bystander interaction, cooperative tasks, and shared control scenarios. It bridges the gap between intent content and communication implementation for transferable agent-human interactions.

## Key Claims
- Intent communication approaches are rigid, agent-specific, and narrowly scoped
- What/how/when to communicate are rarely linked systematically
- Transparency×Abstraction×Modality design space generates cross-domain strategies
- Bridging intent content and implementation enables safer, intuitive interactions

## Key Quotes
> "existing models of what to communicate are rarely linked to systematic choices of how and when to communicate" — gap diagnosis

> "our design space provides a foundation for designing safer, more intuitive, and more transferable agent-human interactions" — contribution
